Kai Kleinbard — Projects
========================

## AI Engineering
Democratizing AI systems for everyone, everywhere.
Global initiative supported by Harvard Edge AI Computer Lab and partners (Arduino, Seeed).
Bridges the skills gap in high-performance computing with an end-to-end open-access curriculum.
- Goal: Educate one million AI Engineers by 2030
- Curriculum: MLSysBook (ML Systems textbook), TinyTorch labs (rebuilding PyTorch from scratch), hardware kits
- Community: Monthly Show & Tell forums, Applied AI Engineering Workshops for real-world TinyML projects
- Accessibility: Focused on under-resourced regions, open-source tools for ML on low-power microcontrollers

## KAIK.SPACE
High-fidelity spatial augmentation and WebGL experiment lab.
Creative portfolio and experimental playground pushing browser-based 3D graphics boundaries.
- Visual Tech Stack: Three.js, React + Vite, Anime.js — cloth animations, parallax effects, gradients
- Experimental Features: Spatial augmentation, WebGL video distortions, WebGL image effects
- Interactive Components: AI-enabled live chat, snap-to-fit UI experiments
- Performance: Lenis smooth scrolling, Framer Motion fluid transitions across 3D scenes

## Arch-Scholar
Agentic AI research system for scholarly paper review.
Developed at Harvard Edge AI Computer Lab. Multi-agent framework automating academic peer-review.
Uses "dragonfly eyes" approach — multiple specialized agents analyzing research simultaneously.
- Architecture: Hierarchical multi-agent system, primary agents + sub-agents in parallel
- Scholarly RAG: Retrieval-Augmented Generation against massive research corpus
- Automated Reviewing: Rigorous scholarly reviews for academic festivals and conferences
- Parallel Synthesis: Maps, synthesizes, monitors multiple research narratives at once
